The Bubble Still Floats

April, 2025

The Global Game Jam Hong Kong 2025 was officially ended. I heard about Global Game Jam Next for the first time from the Global Game Jam site when I tried to search "Hong Kong", then the "Next" game jam appeared on the search engine. This one is a five-day game jam for university level, so it gives more flexibility and less stressful than the 40-hour game jam.

At first, I had no intention of joining since I always wanted to make games as a team to make the experience meaningful and get a better quality. Also, the date was a tough day due to my university assignments were just too much to complete in that time, including the Unreal Engine project that I have been doing for the past few months. Him asked me to come join the game jam, but I said no because of that, and I was also afraid to not being able to contribute that much to the team as my workload is already too heavy.

I also joined the game jam's Discord server just for fun and to see how it goes, possibly for making some connections too. The chat wasn't very active as the Global Game Jam server.

Day 1, Monday

It's the D-Day for the game jam to start and it started off with the opening ceremony which takes place on HKIIT or IVE. I watched the livestream on YouTube as I have some spare time on campus. I was kinda confused with the given theme which was actually the same with the Global Game Jam, and that is "Bubble".

"Why do they have the same theme?" I ponder...

And the rules said that I can just submit the Global Game Jam submission if the game hasn't won any awards yet, which makes it legal. I didn't do it because it felt unfair and also knowing Him is doing the game jam with different game. So I pass.

Day 2, Tuesday

The group chat becomes really active for the first time. People started talking and asking for any collaborations for the game jam. Some people were struggling to find teammate that can do arts.

I asked about how many people are there in the game jam and it's almost the same as the global game jam, plus it has more people if combined with the high schoolers if total.

Day 3, Wednesday

My schedule wasn't too bad so I started to think of joining the game jam.

I suddenly had an idea of trying to use some mechanism like the ones on Global Game Jam. Computer Vision, multiplayer, voice control, etc.

Then I thought about tilting or rotating the phone as the main control.

Don't pop the bubble!

The idea came up after joining Global Game Jam where we made a game about bubble sort which requires players to pop the bubbles to reveal the randomized numbers. I thought about the opposite of not popping the bubbles as contrast.

Then I decided to join the game jam late.

Started quick prototype first with Unity and managed to make the input system working.

Day 4, Thursday

Started to put more obstacles like spikes and bombs, also some blocking components. Also added borders to prevent going out of screen.

Day 5, Friday

Added more fun, trying to adjust the increasing difficulty as time goes. Added score system. Then make a video and sleep.

Day 6, Saturday, Presentation and Award Ceremony

Surprised to see the rewards are the same as Global Game Jam. There are PS5, Switch, Board games, etc.

Presentation and awards split to two groups: high school and university.

Presentation process is just showing 1-minute video and you can either stay silent or talk a bit about your game.

Saw the high school projects and seems that they are more fully committed into making cool games.

After that, announcements. I managed to won the Best Overall award.

Very unexpected win for me.

My thoughts about the Global Game Jam Next

 Difficulty is much easier compared to Global Game Jam.

Key Points that I have learned from Global Game Jam Next

Make it fun and well-polished

My thoughts about the Cathay Hackathon

My personal thought that the 2023 hackathon was better compared 2024 hackathon since the 2023 hackathon was more collaborative with coaches and other teams which was really useful for the development and the environment seems to be more fun and supportive as well.

I don't have much thoughts on the systems of the hackathon. The foods they provided were nice. The prize is also good.

The only criticism I have would be the balance of fairness on the support from the coaches. There are significant support gap between our 2023 coach which was really infomative and well-distributed in handling all teams compared to our 2024 coach which was lack of information and only focuses on one team by abandoning the other teams.

But overall, it was a really good experience for everyone who wants to have a better portfolio and also develop teamwork abilities and responsibilities as like working in companies. It's also a good time to connect with different people and experts, especially if you want to work in Cathay Pacific then it's worth joining.

Key Points that I have learned from Cathay Hackathon

Presentation is highly important. You are trying to do a business presentation on the product you have, not showing what you did during the development period.

To make a good team, you team chemistry is highly important to make working synergy more effective as team communication is important to put everyone into the same idea. It's not easy if you want to win the hackathon while you aren't familiar with your teammates yet. So, it's better to go to this hackathon with your friends.

You also need to know what they can do the best for the hackathon. For our case, we had one mainly as the pitcher, one focused on building pitch PowerPoint, one working on the front end, and two working on the back end.

FYI, if you want to get featured in Cathay's post articles or videos about the hackathon, make sure not to cover your green Cathay hackathon shirts :)

Why did I join the Cathay Hackathon twice?

I joined the Cathay Hackathon for the second time to experience it again. The best part is working in a team. It's really fun to work in a team with passionate members. It's the best feeling for me, as it feels like a dream.

Friendship and teamwork make dream work.

Seeing From a New Perspective

June, 2024

I have made a lot of games.

However, there is one thing that I'm missing in my portfolio.

Working for others.

It's a really critical thing to have in portfolio which can better prepare you for working in a company in adults life. Starting from intern, full-time, etc.

From the past few years, I did help others projects.

But I felt like it's not quite enough experience to earn due to several reasons.

1. It's just a duo/trio devs project.
For me, working on a really small team doesn't really give much experience on having to commit my desire on working on a game.

2. Lone programmer starting from scratch.
If you work alone as a programmer of the game which no one can give feedbacks on how I code.

3. No enough passion.
Mostly just for fun.

4. No trust build. In fact, I was scammed twice.
No funding and clear payment is hard.

Getting Started

How I try to find is through Roblox's Talent Hub, Discord community servers, and Developer Hub game in Roblox.

Lufo contacted me from Developer Hub. But I was AFK that time, at least I put a sign of my Discord username in case I went AFK, so he contacted me through Discord.

Asked me to do AI pathfinding. I agreed.

Asked me to do other ad-hoc tasks. Made me lead developer.

Development Time

Group has around 6-10 people.

Has another programmer, Yoricle. But he wasn't that active on the group.

Did AI pathfinding, more complex than I thought.

Did some other stuffs: puzzles, matchmaking, etc.

Then Yoricle got active, then completed some other programming tasks that I haven't finished it yet.

Payed attention on how he scripted, clearly different style from me. Usually I create multiple script objects since it's faster and easier for me to code. Drawback is the potential spagethi code which is hard to maintain. While him uses modulescripts where you can put many functions that sometimes gets triggered for the same thing, similar like Unity or Unreal Engine scripting method.

Slow Down

No more task. Lufo demoted me to contributor role since there's no more job, and Yoricle became the lead project.

Didn't follow up that often on the progress.

Some members leave or demoted to contributor role.

No clear directions on the game. No clear release date.

I don't know what happened on the team.

My assumption is:

1. Not reached satisfaction point.

2. No real development experience.

3. No motivation. Payments are still unclear.

Key Points that I have learned from Kaiko

Made me realize the importance of writing clean code through object-oriented programming style.

Made me also realize that what I've been doing is just handling too many projects which leads to misfocus on the task I need to do. Made me self committed to just focus on 1-2 projects at a time.

Side Quest: Dreamers Never Die

September-November, 2023

The first time I heard about the hackathon was from my friend Richie. He asked me to join the hackathon as a team alongside his friend Jam. Later, we invited our friend Dorj to hop on the team. Several days later, my summer intern friend Kendrew was looking for a team so I asked him too.

At first, our lineup was solid. By the time we joined the hackathon, Jam was a senior with prior knowledge from his other previous hackathon. Richie had a summer internship experience in a startup company working on a Flutter app. Dorj had extensive experience in Flutter and was strong in competitive programming. Kendrew did some personal front-end projects and had experience in creating UI/UX on Figma. I have a strong background in game development and have done many projects so I know how to create well-polished projects. I also have video-making experience using Adobe Premiere Pro, and Canva for creating pitch PowerPoint.

During the registration stage, we met to fill out the registration form together and try to come up with a decent idea since the form requires us to do so. Although it’s just for registration, we spent quite a while on the idea because we didn’t know much about the criteria to go through the next stage.

Initial Submission Stage

After making it through the registration stage, Cathay gave us several problem statements that we could choose (Premium Lifestyle, Travel Experience, and Cargo) and also access to the Microsoft Teams account to connect with Cathay people. That is where I also found out our opponents and found out that there are a total of around 550 participants making it through the initial submission stage, meaning that there should be around 110 – 183 teams in total, which is quite a lot.

I tried to analyze how Cathay Hackathon works by searching the past years. When I checked the Cathay Pacific news about the year-2022 hackathon, I found out that there were only 20 teams invited for the development day. So I assumed that it would be the same for that year (which I was wrong later), I predicted the chance for us to go through the initial submission stage was only around 10-18%, so we had to do well.

With that low probability, we tried to spend several days to come up with several ideas until we found a really good idea. However, we couldn’t come up with an idea that could be agreed by all five of us until two days before the initial submission deadline. We had a group meeting and decided to proceed with the best idea we had and then proceed to make the poster and the presentation video.

Pre-Development Day

Several days later, we got the result that we were qualified for the next stage. We later had the opportunity to chat with our coach Enoch Wang, a solution specialist in Cathay Pacific. We asked about what to expect on the development day.

On development day, I found quite a lot of my friends also doing the hackathon, so my thoughts were really impressive. Later, our coach told us that there are 60 teams in total, 20 teams in each category, I was like “Oh…” kinda surprised me since the total number of teams is three times more than the total in 2022.

Development Day Hour 1-2

For the first hour (10 AM), our coach brought us to our room and explained what to do during the Hackathon. He also told us that only six teams will go through the final stage, so it means the chance for us would be 10%.

Then we had lunch. We had lunch with our coach and talked about some of our ideas. However, our coach gave the feedback that most of the ideas we have are not feasible and not unique enough. He told us to think about something that Cathay hadn’t done.

I mentioned an app to help blind people navigate in the airport, an idea that I had thought about a day before the development day. Our coach said that the idea was okay. Our team then tried to expand the idea into an app to help disabled people travel with ease, which not only helps blind people. He mentioned that Cathay hadn’t done that kind of idea, so our team managed to agree on one idea for the first time.

Development Day Hour 3-6

We spent most of the time researching about our idea.

We also went to meet the experts who are available for Q&As during the first six hours of the development day. We asked for some opinions about our current ideas to some experts. One of the experts mentioned that Cathay hadn’t done that idea before.

At the same time, there are some optional fun sessions for teams to come and attend but we didn't attend them so we have more time to work on our solution.

After doing more research, we reached back to our coach to get more opinions.

Development Day Hour 7-14

After getting enough feedback and research, we started the development by creating a working app prototype using Flutter. Dorj, Richie, and Jam immediately set up the core programs. Kendrew started creating UI mockups for developer reference and for the PowerPoint pitch. I started to create the PowerPoint pitch using Canva. When I had nothing to put on the PowerPoint, I joined the development team and worked on the chatbot system integrated with OpenAI LLM.

Development Day Hour 15-18

After hours of development, we felt something off with our idea because it didn’t seem to be innovative enough. To solve that issue, we made some changes to our original idea by implementing new features.

Dorj gave me some input on the PowerPoint which allows me to have clear direction. Most of my teammates fell asleep to gain more energy to complete the prototype and prepare for the pitch. I didn't sleep because I needed to finish the PowerPoint perfectly.

Development Day Hour 19-24

All of our members were awake and started wrapping up the prototype. Turns out, there were some difficulties in solving one particular problem which was quite critical for the innovation point. One of us focused on solving it while the rest worked on polishing and checking the PowerPoint, including filling out the one-pager.

First Pitch

After the 24-hour development day was concluded (10 AM). We first had breakfast. Then, we went back to our room to prepare for our pitch. We only had about 3 hours to prepare.

Originally, the pitching lineup consisted of Jam as the main pitcher, Richie as the second pitcher, and me as the controller of the PowerPoint. Due to some of our lack of sleep and energy, we had a small line-up change into Jam as the main pitcher explaining the problem statement and the solution, Dorj as the second pitcher explaining the tech stacks, and Richie controlling the PowerPoint. We had some practices several times with our coach leaving some feedback for our perfect pitch, including some small adjustments on the PowerPoint to ensure the pitcher was able to keep the pitch flow well.

Right before our turn, we were waiting at the front of the door of the pitching room. A Cathay person (probably the coach of the other team) approached us and looked at our pitch slides. I showed him our slides as I was holding the laptop for the pitch, and he said "There's a team that has a similar idea as yours, but you have nice PowerPoint." At first, we quite panicked but still keep ourselves calm and do our best.

We even had a small shout of "3.. 2.. 1.. Skydreamers!" to motivated ourselves a bit.

When we walked inside the pitching room, there were about 10 judges. About 7 of them were around middle ages and the other 3 seemed quite young. There were also a moderator that controlled the time for us to pitch for 5 minutes and the other 5 minutes to do a Q&A session. Our pitch went smoothly and ended before overtime. The Q&A lasted long for 5 minutes as the judges kept asking questions about either the technical or the extensibility of our app.

After we finished our first pitch, we became less energetic, tired, and hopeless, pondering whether our work was good enough to make it into the top six teams. We packed up our belongings until the result announcements announced our group number and we were shocked to hear that in disbelief. We made it through the final stage.

Final Pitch

We were given about 1-2 hour to prepare for the final pitch. We tried to fix some codes on the prototype in case of needed to demonstrate the prototype. We kept the same plan as how we did on the first pitch but focuses more on the business side instead of the technical side as there will be more judges watching our presentation and most of them weren't that specific into the technical stuff that we were doing.

Award Ceremony

We were quite satisfied with our hard work. We were surprised to win the Best Developer Award and Best Innovation Award too as well. The most important thing is that we won 2nd Runner-up. It was my first time doing a hackathon and somehow ended up beating the other 1500 participants by winning the second Runner-up and those other awards. The hackathon changed my perspective on being pessimistic on doing competitions to wanting to work hard again.

Post Cathay Hackathon 2023

After the hackathon ended, we were invited by Cathay to the conference to showcase our ideas, as well as a special collaboration between Cathay x Skydreamers to make our idea into real life solution. I'm sure this also works for the winner and the first runner-up. We also have ourselves being featured on HKU's Instagram since we were the only group with only HKU students to win second Runner-up on Cathay Hackathon.

Pre Development (Summer 2022)

There were two factors that motivated me to make this game.

First factor, that time I was developing Infinite Obby. Then, I got bored a bit and wanted to make something new to relieve my boredom in making games. A quick game project that I can release it in less than a week.

Second factor, it was almost the time after my high school graduation, meaning its before I started university. I want to go to Computer Science.

For getting the Computer Science major on the 2nd year, I noticed that there's a quota.

I did panicked a bit on my skills that may be not enough as Hong Kong University is also known for its competitiveness and there must be a lot of smart people. But still chose HKU since it would be good to improve my programming skills so I can make better games quick. So I wanted to study early before going to university. Studied some year one courses. Saw some courses related to AI.

I tried learning different programming languages beside lua, like Python and a little bit of C++. To further develop my skills, I thought of trying to create a chatbot as a coding project since having project is useful to develop our skills fast.

That time I have no knowledge on how to make AI, or even what is LLM. What I did first is just followed the Python programming tutorial on YouTube. Uses the Key Matching algorithm.

Then, somehow had an idea to do it on Roblox since Roblox games on have NPC packed with dialogue system where players just choose 1-3 dialogue options to chat. So it's just scripted chatbot, while the Python tutorial doesn't do that way. So I wanted to try doing it so I can improve my programming skills even better.

My Thoughts and Key Lessons

Had a really hard time just to work hard on getting more players.

As a consequence, I focused more on how to get players without thinking effectively on how to make a fresh and new game concept like The Talking NPC, a game where you can that with a NPC freely like ChatGPT.

Following a trend surely does help, while creating a new trend is hard and risky, but has a chance for being viral and easily recognized by more players.

Sticking on the same game for years is just waste of time if the game doesn't do well. Start a new better game is what's the best that we could do to not waste potential.
